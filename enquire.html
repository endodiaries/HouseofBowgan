Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

A few things our clients commonly ask before getting started.

How far in advance should I contact you?

+

For weddings, we recommend reaching out at least 6–12 months in advance to ensure availability and allow enough time for thorough planning. For other events, 2–4 months is usually ideal, though we are sometimes able to accommodate shorter timelines depending on availability.

Do you only plan weddings?

+

No — while weddings are our speciality and our heart, we plan a full range of celebratory events including birthdays, anniversaries, surprise parties, end-of-year parties, fundraisers, and galas. If it’s worth celebrating, we’d love to be part of it.

Can you work within my budget?

+

Yes. We work with a range of budgets and believe that luxury is about intentionality, not just spend. During our initial consultation, we will have an honest conversation about your budget and what is achievable within it. Our job is to make every naira count.

Do you travel outside Port Harcourt?

+

Absolutely. We are based in Port Harcourt but available to plan and coordinate events across Nigeria — Lagos, Abuja, Enugu, Calabar, and beyond — as well as international and destination events. Travel and logistics costs are discussed transparently during the proposal stage.

What happens after I submit this form?

+

We will review your enquiry personally and reach out within 48 hours to schedule a discovery call. This is a relaxed, no-obligation conversation where we learn more about your vision and you get a feel for how we work. From there, we send a bespoke proposal tailored to your event.

What is the P.I.Y service and is it right for me?

+

P.I.Y (Plan It Yourself) is our guided planning service for clients who want to be hands-on with their event but need expert support behind them. You remain the creative director while we provide planning frameworks, vendor access, strategy sessions, and on-call guidance. It’s ideal for someone who loves being involved but doesn’t want to be overwhelmed.
